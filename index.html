Leonardo Da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci, the Italian polymath of the Renaissance.


the man himself


Here's a time-line of Da Vinci's life:


  • 1452 - Leonardo da Vinci is born on April 15, 1452, in Vinci, a town in the Republic of Florence. Born out of wedlock to Ser Piero da Vinci and a peasant woman named Caterina, Leonardo grows up in Vinci and later moves to Florence.

  • 1466 - Around the age of 14, da Vinci becomes an apprentice in the workshop of the renowned artist Andrea del Verrocchio in Florence. Here, he learns various artistic skills, including painting, sculpting, and drafting.

  • 1472 - Leonardo becomes a member of the Guild of St. Luke, a prestigious guild for artists and doctors in Florence, indicating his recognition as a master artist.

  • 1482 - Leonardo moves to Milan and enters the service of Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan. He works on various projects, including engineering, architectural designs, and artwork for the Sforza court.

  • 1490 - Leonardo completes his masterpiece, "The Last Supper," a mural painting located in the Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie in Milan. The painting depicts the moment Jesus announces that one of his disciples will betray him.

  • 1495 - Da Vinci creates the iconic "Vitruvian Man," a pen and ink drawing accompanied by notes based on the writings of the Roman architect Vitruvius. The drawing illustrates the proportions of the human body.

  • 1498 - Ludovico Sforza is overthrown by French forces, and Leonardo leaves Milan for Venice. He subsequently returns to Florence.

  • 1503 - Da Vinci begins painting the "Mona Lisa," which becomes one of the most famous and recognizable paintings in the world. He continues working on it until around 1517.

  • 1508 - Leonardo begins a series of detailed anatomical studies, dissecting both humans and animals. His drawings and observations demonstrate a deep understanding of human anatomy and physiology.

  • 1513 - Leonardo moves to Rome and starts working for Giuliano de' Medici, focusing on architectural and engineering projects. However, he faces challenges in completing some of his proposed designs.

  • 1516 - King Francis I of France invites Leonardo to live in the Château du Clos Lucé near the king's residence in Amboise. Leonardo brings with him many notebooks filled with sketches, scientific diagrams, and observations.

  • 1519 - Leonardo da Vinci dies on May 2, 1519, at the Château du Clos Lucé. He is buried in the Chapel of Saint-Hubert in Amboise, France.
